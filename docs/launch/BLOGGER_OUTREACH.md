# Blogger / Newsletter Outreach

Motion's audience splits across **PKM bloggers** (high-fit) and **AI integration bloggers** (MCP angle).

## Template

> **Subject:** `Quick share — open-source Notion alternative with AI agents via MCP (Motion)`
>
> Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},
>
> I read your piece on **{{SPECIFIC_RECENT_POST}}** — {{ONE_GENUINE_SENTENCE_OF_REACTION}}. The bit about {{SPECIFIC_DETAIL}} resonated.
>
> I just open-sourced **Motion** ([github.com/jiusanzhou/motion](https://github.com/jiusanzhou/motion)) — a pure-frontend Notion alternative where notes live in your GitHub repo. The unusual angle: there's a built-in MCP WebSocket server, so any agent that speaks Model Context Protocol (Claude Desktop, Cursor, Cline, custom) can list / read / write / search your notes directly.
>
> If you're considering a piece on **{{TOPIC_THAT_FITS}}**, I'd love to send:
> - A 2-minute walkthrough video (or screenshare a 10-min demo)
> - The architecture write-up (why GitHub-as-DB, why MCP, why pure frontend)
> - Early access to the hosted version (if/when that exists)
>
> Live demo with no signup: motion.wencai.app
>
> No expectation — just thought it might be useful for your readers.
>
> Cheers,
> Zoe
> Maintainer, Motion

## Realistic conversion

- 10 cold emails to PKM bloggers → 2–3 responses → 0–1 piece written
- Newsletter submissions → blind drop, binary outcome
- Anthropic MCP team tagging on launch day Twitter → high-value but not controllable
- YouTubers → very long lead time (weeks to months between contact and video)

**The single most valuable outreach for Motion:** if you can get **one well-written piece by a PKM blogger** with a working demo embedded, that piece will keep sending traffic for *years*. Worth investing in 5 personalized emails over 50 blast emails.
